Battle of Gettysburg

The Battle of Gettysburg began when General Robert E. Leemarched his Army of Northern Virginia into Pennsylvania in late June 1863. On July 1st, the Confederates and the Union army of General George G. Meade had a small battle at the crossroads of Gettysburg. The next day, heavy fighting began when the Confederates attacked the Union from both the left and the right. On July 3, Lee ordered an attack by fewer than 15,000 troops on the enemy’s center at Cemetery Ridge. This attack did get through the Federal’s line, but it soon failed and this resulted in a very large amount of rebel casualties. Lee then withdrew his army on July 4, and resulted in the Union winning the war. The Federals lost about 23,000 men while the Confederates lost about 28,000. This was the bloodiest battle of the Civil War, and the battle that ended it.
